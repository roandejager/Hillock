# Contributing to Hillock 🧠

Hi! Thanks for checking out the project. **Hillock** is currently a work in progress, and honestly, it isn't all that. It's just a personal experiment exploring alternative memory architectures. Because of that, I want to keep the contribution process as simple, friendly, and transparent as possible.

## The Dual-Licensing Model & Our CLA
The core of Hillock is open-source and free under the **AGPL-3.0 license**. Anyone can use it, modify it, and run it locally. 

However, to keep this project sustainable and preserve our ability to dual-license the software commercially to companies who cannot open-source their code under AGPL restrictions, we require all contributors to sign our Contributor License Agreement (CLA) before we can merge your Pull Requests.

Signing the CLA does **not** take away your copyright—you still own your code. It simply grants us the legal permission to distribute your work under both the open-source AGPL-3.0 license and our proprietary commercial licenses.

## How to Contribute
1. Fork the repository and create your branch from `master`.
2. Make your code changes and ensure your local tests run cleanly.
3. Open a Pull Request on GitHub.
4. Our automated CLA bot (via `cla-assistant.io`) will automatically comment on your PR with a link to sign the agreement. Once signed, your PR will be cleared for review!

Thank you for helping make this experiment better!
